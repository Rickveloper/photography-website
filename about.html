About Rich

The Photographer

Landscape photography from coastal Maine—fog, nor'easters, and four brutal, beautiful seasons. Based in York, Maine, Rich has spent over a decade documenting the ever-changing moods of the Atlantic coastline.

His work focuses on the intersection of natural forces and human presence in these marginal spaces, where the ocean meets the granite, and where weather becomes the primary subject.

The Process

Using primarily a Sony a6300, Rich's photographic approach combines technical precision with an intuitive response to weather and light conditions. Each image is a collaboration between photographer and environment, often requiring hours of waiting for the right moment.

The work emphasizes patience, persistence, and a deep respect for the power and unpredictability of the Atlantic coast. Every photograph tells a story of time, weather, and place.

The Mission

Through his photography, Rich seeks to capture not just the visual beauty of coastal Maine, but the emotional and physical experience of being present in these dramatic landscapes.

The goal is to share the raw power and subtle beauty of the Atlantic coast, creating images that resonate with both locals who know these places intimately and visitors who are experiencing them for the first time.

Equipment & Approach

Primary Gear

  • Sony a6300
  • 24-70mm f/2.8 GM
  • 16-35mm f/4 G
  • Gitzo Carbon Tripod

Philosophy

"The best camera is the one you have with you, but the best photographs come from being present, patient, and willing to embrace the elements."

Every shoot is an adventure into the unknown, where preparation meets opportunity and technical skill serves artistic vision.
